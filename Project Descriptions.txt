Timeline: Feb 2025 – May 2025
Role: Mechanical Designer & Structural Analyst
Overview
Developed and analyzed a fully functional miniature wind turbine featuring a 3-blade rotor, 16-inch tower, and enclosed motor housing.
Design Process
                  * Modeled aerodynamic blades, tower, and housing in SolidWorks.

                  * Researched blade pitches, angles of attack, and airfoil profiles to optimize performance.

                  * Applied FEA to refine the stiffness-to-weight ratio and ensure structural stability at high RPM.

                     * RPM: 5354 rpm

                     * Voltage: 3.38 V

Outcome
                        * Produced a strong, cost-efficient turbine with measurable power generation.

                        * Demonstrated direct impact of blade geometry and stiffness on electrical output.

⭐ Wheel Center Design Project
Tools: SolidWorks, FEA, DFM, CNC Machining, Materials Selection
Timeline: (Add dates if needed)
 Role: Sole Mechanical Designer & Analyst
Slideshow: Gabriel Rayos - Drivetrain Presentation
Overview
Designed a lightweight aluminum wheel center capable of withstanding high torsional and lateral loads while maintaining manufacturability and structural safety. The final design met weight, strength, stiffness, and factor-of-safety requirements while optimized for 3-axis CNC machining.
Problem
Develop a wheel center that:
                                 * Weighs under 0.33 lbs

                                 * Maintains ≥1.3 Factor of Safety

                                 * Achieves ≤0.5 mm displacement

                                 * Withstands peak loads of:

                                    * 687 lbf torque

                                    * 185 lbf (Y-direction)

                                    * 296 lbf (Z-direction)

This required balancing structural rigidity, geometry optimization, material selection, and manufacturability constraints.
Design Process
                                       * Modeled a lightweight 8-spoke wheel center with load paths optimized for torque and bending stresses.

                                       * Selected 7075-T6 Aluminum for its high specific strength and excellent machinability.

                                       * Integrated critical features including fillets, lug holes, and weight-reduction slots.

                                       * Positioned lug holes strategically to handle torque transfer and minimize stress risers.

                                       * Applied DFM principles to ensure all features could be manufactured on a 3-axis CNC mill.

                                       * Used GD&T and tolerancing to define critical fits and lug-pattern alignment.

                                       * Iteratively refined geometry—adjusting spoke thickness, chamfers, and pocketing—to reduce weight while maintaining structural performance.

Stress Analysis & Technical Work
                                          * Performed FEA to evaluate Von Mises stress, deflection, and load distribution across spokes and the hub region.

                                          * Analyzed the effects of torque, lateral loads, and combined loading conditions.

                                          * Conducted optimization cycles:

                                             * Removed non-load-bearing material

                                             * Increased fillet radii to reduce stress concentrations

                                             * Adjusted cutout geometry to improve stiffness-to-weight ratio

                                                * Verified manufacturability: cutter access, tool approach vectors, minimum fillet radii, and clamping considerations.

Results
                                                   * Weight: 0.32994 lbs (met target of <0.33 lbs)

                                                   * Factor of Safety: 1.377 (exceeded 1.3 requirement)

                                                   * Maximum Displacement: 0.6819 mm (slightly above 0.5 mm target but structurally acceptable under peak loading)

                                                   * Material: 7075-T6 Aluminum

                                                   * Design: 8-spoke with optimized cutouts, fillets, and lug pattern

Outcome
                                                      * Delivered a high-performance wheel center balancing strength, manufacturability, and weight optimization.

                                                      * Demonstrated ability to design for real-world loading scenarios through FEA, iterative refinement, and material-driven engineering judgment.

                                                      * Gained strong understanding of structural load interaction in wheels, CNC constraints, and optimization-driven design.
⭐ FSAE Tripod Housing Development — Inboard & Outboard Joints (In Progress)
Tools: ANSYS, SolidWorks, Lathe + CNC + EDM Manufacturing, Materials Research
Timeline: Fall 2025 – Present
Role: Manufacturing Lead & Design Research Engineer (Drivetrain Subteam)
Overview
This ongoing project involves understanding, manufacturing, and eventually redesigning the inboard and outboard tripod housings used in our FSAE drivetrain. I am responsible for leading the part’s fabrication while developing a deep understanding of load paths, contact mechanics, Hertzian stresses, and the failure modes that govern future design decisions.
Problem
The tripod housings must withstand extreme torque and impact loads transmitted through the driveshaft while maintaining low friction and predictable compliance. With a new engine upgrade introducing higher peak loads, the existing housing design may become insufficient. My goal is to understand the current design at a detailed level so I can confidently redesign it when new load conditions are finalized.
Design Research
                                                         * Analyzed the current tripod housing geometry to understand load transfer between the tripod trunnions and the bearing surfaces.

                                                         * Studied Hertzian contact theory to understand how deformation and contact pressure influence wear and fatigue.

                                                         * Investigated bolt preload and compression forces on the housing structure, identifying regions most sensitive to clamping and shear stresses.

                                                         * Evaluated surface interactions between the tripod rollers and housing channels, noting the sensitivity of contact patch geometry to small changes in radius, width, and surface finish.

Simulation Work
                                                            * Learned to run structural simulations in ANSYS, including:

                                                               * Hertzian contact stress analyses

                                                               * Bolt preload & torque clamp simulations

                                                               * Stress distributions through the housing ribs and webs

                                                               * Displacement and deformation under worst-case torque

                                                                  * Established a baseline FEA workflow for future redesigns, including meshing strategies, boundary conditions, and convergence checks.

Manufacturing Leadership
                                                                     * Serving as Manufacturing Lead for the current year’s tripod housings.

                                                                     * Responsible for full fabrication pipeline:

                                                                        * Lathe machining for turning operations

                                                                        * CNC machining for complex pockets, spline interfaces, and contours

                                                                        * EDM for precision slotting, tight tolerances, and internal geometries

                                                                           * Coordinated manufacturing constraints with design decisions, ensuring the part remains feasible to fabricate within team resources.

Future Design Intent
                                                                              * Preparing to redesign the tripod housing if the upgraded engine introduces higher torque or lateral loads.

                                                                              * Building knowledge to adjust:

                                                                                 * Contact surface profiles (radius, width, chamfer geometry)

                                                                                 * Rib thickness and support structure

                                                                                 * Material selection & heat treatment

                                                                                 * Internal clearances and roller travel paths

                                                                                 * Bolt patterns, torque distribution, and web stiffening

                                                                                    * Goal: Produce a next-gen housing with improved fatigue life, lower Hertzian stress, and manufacturability optimized for our student shop’s capabilities.

Outcome (In Progress)
                                                                                       * Developed a comprehensive understanding of the tripod housing’s function, loads, and wear mechanisms.

                                                                                       * Established a reliable simulation workflow for validating future design changes.

                                                                                       * Leading the machining and production of the current housing with Lathe + CNC + EDM workflows.

                                                                                       * Positioned to take full ownership of the redesigned housing next season when new load conditions are released.

⭐ Acoustic Holography Research — Piezoelectric Transducer Experimental Setup (In Progress)
Tools: SolidWorks, 3D Printing, Acrylic Fabrication, PCB Integration, Experimental Design
Affiliation: Liwei Lin Lab, UC Berkeley
 Timeline: Fall 2025 – Present
Role: Undergraduate Researcher (Experimental Design & Prototyping)
Overview
This project explores the creation of acoustic holograms using piezoelectric transducers to manipulate and pattern biological cells suspended in water. I am designing the experimental platform that will support future hologram testing, cell imaging, and acoustic field validation.
Problem
To study acoustic holography, the lab needs a stable, modular experimental system that:
                                                                                                      * Precisely positions a petri dish above a PCB-mounted piezoelectric transducer

                                                                                                      * Holds a water-filled acoustic tank with optically clear walls

                                                                                                      * Maintains alignment between transducer, water column, and sample

                                                                                                      * Supports integration of custom 3D-printed acoustic holograms

                                                                                                      * Minimizes mechanical noise and allows for repeatable, controlled testing

Design Process
                                                                                                         * Created a 3D-printed base platform with mounting features for the PCB board and piezoelectric transducer.

                                                                                                         * Designed a petri-dish holder that maintains consistent height and alignment during imaging.

                                                                                                         * Modeled the acrylic tank walls for the water bath, focusing on optical clarity, rigidity, and low distortion for imaging.

                                                                                                         * Considered constraints such as water depth, standing-wave formation, imaging angle, and wave attenuation.

                                                                                                         * Ensured modularity of all components to allow rapid redesign as experimental conditions evolve.

Current Work
                                                                                                            * Integrating the base assembly with the transducer PCB and verifying mechanical alignment.

                                                                                                            * Preparing the water tank and evaluating optical characteristics for imaging cell responses.

                                                                                                            * Running preliminary trials to observe how cells move in response to the acoustic field.

Next Steps (Design + Research Direction)
                                                                                                               * Design a custom 3D-printed acoustic hologram (phase plate) to shape the emitted acoustic wavefront.

                                                                                                               * Test whether the hologram produces a target field distribution and desired cell spatial patterns.

                                                                                                               * Iterate hologram geometries based on observed cell behavior and measured acoustic fields.

                                                                                                               * Explore multi-layer hologram designs, frequency tuning, and material modifications.

Outcome (In Progress)
                                                                                                                  * Developed the foundational experimental infrastructure for acoustic hologram testing.

                                                                                                                  * Enabled controlled visualization of cell patterns formed by acoustic fields.

                                                                                                                  * Gaining experience in wave mechanics, experimental design, PCB integration, and bio-acoustic imaging.
